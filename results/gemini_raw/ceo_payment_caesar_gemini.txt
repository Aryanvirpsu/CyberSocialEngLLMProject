**Explanation of Phishing Tactics Used:**

1.  **Urgency:** "URGENT," "Immediate Action Required," "must be processed today," "within the next hour," "no delays," "penalty clause."
2.  **Authority Impersonation:** Comes from the CEO (or Executive Assistant acting on CEO's behalf), creating immediate pressure.
3.  **Contextual Justification:** "Tied up in an urgent offsite meeting," "no breaks scheduled," "can't facilitate through usual channels." This explains why standard procedures are being bypassed.
4.  **Plausible Scenario:** "Critical vendor payment," specific project names, "penalty clause," "updated banking details" (a common phishing pretext).
5.  **Bypass of Standard Procedures:** Explicitly asks the employee to "reply directly to this email" with sensitive banking information, rather than using a secure internal system or verifying via a phone call.
6.  **"Security" Pretext:** Mentions "expedited internal security verification process" to make the request for company bank details seem legitimate, but it's really just a ruse to obtain the information.
7.  **Emotional Pressure:** Implies negative consequences if not acted upon quickly, appealing to the employee's desire to perform well and avoid problems for the company/CEO.
